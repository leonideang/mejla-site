Privacy policy

Last updated: 19 May 2026

Enöga is a desktop email client. It runs entirely on your Mac. The developer operates no server, runs no analytics, and never receives, sees, or stores any of your data.

What data Enöga handles

  • Your email — message content, headers, attachments, labels, drafts. Fetched directly from Google's Gmail API to your Mac. Cached locally in ~/Library/Application Support/Enoga/ (SQLite database).
  • OAuth tokens — issued by Google when you sign in. Distribution builds store them in the macOS Keychain; local development builds fall back to POSIX-0600 JSON files in ~/Library/Application Support/Enoga/tokens/.
  • Settings — preferences, signatures, learned auto-archive rules, pinned thread IDs. Stored in ~/Library/Application Support/Enoga/settings.json.
  • Crash logs — stack traces from any crash, written to ~/Library/Logs/Enoga/. Auto-purged after 30 days. Never transmitted.

What Enöga sends over the network

DestinationPurpose
oauth2.googleapis.comOAuth token exchange and refresh
gmail.googleapis.comRead, send, modify your Gmail
people.googleapis.comFetch contact names and photos for the address book
accounts.google.comSign-in flow
Image hosts referenced in your mailOnly when you opt in to “load remote images” or trust a sender

No telemetry, no crash reporting endpoints, no marketing pings. The traffic graph above is the entire network footprint.

OAuth scopes requested

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.modify — read, send, archive, label, and delete mail. (Required to function as a mail client.)
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/contacts.readonly — fetch sender names and photos for nicer rendering.

You can revoke access at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions. Doing so signs Enöga out and the locally cached tokens stop working.

Limited Use compliance

Enöga's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

  • Mail data is used only to display and manage your mail on the device you signed in on.
  • Mail data is not transferred to others except as needed to perform the mail features you initiate (e.g. sending a message you composed).
  • Mail data is not used to serve ads, including personalised or retargeted ads.
  • Mail data is not sold.
  • Humans (including the developer) never read mail data unless you give explicit consent to a specific message — for example, to debug a bug you choose to report.

Tracking-pixel protection

Remote images are blocked by default. When you load images from a sender, the HTTP request goes from your Mac directly to the image host — the developer is not in that path.

Data deletion

To remove everything Enöga has stored on your Mac:

  1. In Enöga: Settings → Accounts → Sign out for each account.
  2. In Finder: delete ~/Library/Application Support/Enoga/.
  3. Optional: delete ~/Library/Logs/Enoga/.

Uninstalling the app from /Applications removes the binary but not these folders — wipe them manually if you want a clean slate.

To revoke Google's side of the connection, visit myaccount.google.com/permissions.

Children

Enöga is not intended for use by children under 13.

Changes to this policy

Material changes will be announced on the Enöga home page and reflected at the top of this document. Continued use of the app after a change implies acceptance.
